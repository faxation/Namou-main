Questions we often hear.

Can foreigners buy freehold land in Ras Al Khaimah?

Yes. Since 2005, RAK has designated several freehold zones — including Al Marjan Island, Al Hamra, and Mina Al Arab — where foreign nationals can acquire 100% ownership of land and property. No local sponsor or UAE residency is required.

Reference: Khaleej Times

Can I get a Golden Visa by investing in RAK real estate?

Yes. A property investment of AED 2 million or more in any UAE emirate qualifies you for a 10-year renewable Golden Visa. The property must be fully paid or mortgaged with a significant deposit.

Reference: UAE Government Portal

What taxes apply to rental income and capital gains in RAK?

The UAE levies no personal income tax, no capital gains tax, and no tax on rental income for individuals. Corporate tax of 9% applies only to businesses with profits above AED 375,000.

Reference: PwC Tax Summary

Why invest in RAK instead of Dubai?

RAK offers entry prices significantly below Dubai, higher gross rental yields, and a fast-growing pipeline anchored by Wynn Al Marjan. Freehold ownership, zero income tax, and 45 minutes from Dubai.

Reference: Knight Frank Wealth Report

How is the Wynn Resort affecting property values in Al Marjan?

Wynn Al Marjan — the first Wynn outside the US — is projected to drive significant appreciation across Al Marjan Island. Hospitality-zoned land nearby has seen demand accelerate sharply since construction began.

Reference: Arabian Business
